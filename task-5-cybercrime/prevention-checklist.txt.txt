
Stay Safe Online: A Guide for Students


Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Always use 2FA on your email, GitHub, and LinkedIn accounts.


Inspect Links: Hover over any link in an email to see the actual URL before clicking.


Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Avoid using the same password for your college portal and personal social media.


Verify the Sender: If an email seems urgent or suspicious, contact the office directly through a known phone number or official website.


Update Software Regularly: Keep your browser and operating system updated to patch security vulnerabilities.


Be Wary of Public Wi-Fi: Avoid accessing bank accounts or sensitive portals on unprotected public networks.


UPI Safety - Verify Names: Always double-check the recipient's name in your UPI app before hitting "Pay" or entering your PIN.


UPI Safety - PIN is for Paying: Remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money; if someone asks you to enter it for a "refund," it is a scam.


Reporting Channel: If you encounter or fall victim to a cybercrime, report it immediately at the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline 1930
