I did a task where I had to be very careful with how I wrote emails. I made a guide to help people be safe on social media. This was part of my job as a Student Digital Ambassador.

There is a problem when people do not communicate well online. For example using forms like "u" instead of "you" in emails, to teachers or bosses can cause a lot of trouble. I remember one time a student sent an email to a teacher asking about their grade. The email did not even have a line and it started with "Hey". The student used slang like "u" and "nvm" which's not a good way to write to a teacher.

Because the email was not written properly the teacher did not even bother to reply. This meant the student missed the chance to appeal their grade. To avoid this the student should have written a subject line like "Grade Query. [Name]". Started the email with a proper greeting. The email should have been polite and easy to understand.

This task taught me that how you write and what you say is very important when you are communicating with someone. I also learned that it is very important to be careful what you put online because it can affect your career in the run. When I made the media checklist I saw how important it is to keep your online presence professional. As a Student Digital Ambassador I think this is very important for people to know.
